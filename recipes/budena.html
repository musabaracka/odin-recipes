Budena

Description

Imagine budena as the perfect edible plate! It takes some time to master this flatbread - more widely known as injera.
But when it works out well, you will enjoy yourself with all types of stews poured on top of the budena!

    Ingredients

  • 240 g teff flour
  • 750 + 250 ml + 150 ml (cooked and chilled) water

    Steps

  1. Mix 750 ml water and teff flour.
  2. Cover the bowl with a cheesecloth or plastic wrap.
  3. Let the mixture ferment for 4-5 days or until bubbles have formed on top and the water has separated from the mixture.
  4. Pour out the water on top and stir the mixture.
  5. Cook 250 ml water with 28 ml of the mixture until it is thick. If you use a different amount of water, make sure to stick to the 9:1 ratio of water and fermented dough.
  6. Stir the cooked batter into the mixture with 150 ml water or until the batter is thin and runny.
  7. Pour some of the mixture into a pan and wait for the bubbles to form.
  8. Put the lid on the pan and let the budena steam until it is cooked through.
  9. Plate the budena and go back to step 7 until you have run out of the mixture.
